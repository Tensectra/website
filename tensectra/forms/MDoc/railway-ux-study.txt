RAILWAY.COM - SITE FLOW & UX STUDY
===================================
Date: 2026
Purpose: Understand Railway's information architecture and conversion flow
         to inform Tensectra's own value proposition and customer journey.
Trigger: Developer/lead engineer feedback that Tensectra's value prop is
         unclear and pricing appears too early on the page.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. RAILWAY NAVIGATION STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Top nav order:  Product | Developers | Enterprise | Company | Pricing

This order is deliberate:
  - Product   = what it is and what it does (the "what")
  - Developers = who it is for and how it works for them (the "who")
  - Enterprise = bigger teams, compliance, SLAs (the "scale")
  - Company   = trust signals, story, team (the "why us")
  - Pricing   = cost LAST, only after the visitor already understands value

KEY INSIGHT: Pricing is the last nav item. The user knows the product,
understands the audience fit, has seen enterprise credibility, has read
the company story --- THEN they see pricing. This sequence builds
perceived value before introducing cost.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. RAILWAY HOMEPAGE FLOW (Product Page)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Section order on the homepage:

  a) Hero: "Ship software peacefully" + "The all-in-one intelligent cloud provider"
     - One bold emotional headline (benefit, not feature)
     - One supporting sub-headline (category label)
     - CTA: Deploy -> Demo Deploy

  b) Core Feature Pillars (each with an "Alternative to ..." label):
     1. Build and Deploy   - "Deploy anything without the complexity"
     2. Network and connect - "Instant networking. Zero setup."
     3. Scale and grow      - "Grow big without the growing pains"
     4. Monitor and observe - "Logs, metrics, and alerts in one place."
     5. Evolve and collaborate - "a development workflow that actually flows"

  c) Social proof BEFORE pricing:
     - "Trusted by the best in business"
     - Named quotes with titles (Tech Lead at Bilt, CTO at G2X, etc.)
     - "Join 2M+ developers building with Railway"
     - Tweet-style testimonials from real developers

  d) No pricing on the homepage at all.

KEY INSIGHT: The homepage never shows pricing. It shows the dream state
("ship peacefully"), proves it works (5 named feature pillars), and
validates it with social proof from real engineers and company CTOs.
Price comes only when the visitor actively navigates to Pricing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RAILWAY PRICING PAGE STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Headline: "Pay only for what your app uses, by the second"
  - Sub: "Say goodbye to overprovisioning and optimizing box sizes."

  Tiers:
    Free    $0/mo       - Try it, no card required
    Hobby   $5 min      - Side projects
    Pro     $20 min     - Production teams
    Enterprise Custom   - Scale + compliance

  Usage-based pricing message: "only charged for extra resource usage"
  This removes the fear of overpaying.

KEY INSIGHT: Pricing is framed as SAVINGS ("pay only for what you use")
not as a barrier. The free tier is prominent and no credit card is
required -- this lowers the commitment threshold dramatically. Pricing
feels like a reward, not a gate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. RAILWAY ENTERPRISE PAGE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Headline: "Loved by developers at 31% of the F500"
  Sub: "Enterprise-grade reliability and compliance, developer-grade experience"

  Trust metrics shown:
    - 5,000,000+ Public Railway services
    - 10,000,000+ Monthly deployments
    - 1+ Trillion Edge network requests
    - 99.99% SLA
    - 50ms p95 Global Network RTT

  Social proof: Named CTOs and VPs with specific ROI statements:
    - "Our bill is reduced by at least 75% and with the devex, it is

    - "Services that took 1 week to configure elsewhere take 1 day

KEY INSIGHT: Enterprise page leads with proof of adoption at scale,
not feature checklists. Engineers trust numbers and peer stories
more than marketing language.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. WHAT TENSECTRA CAN LEARN FROM THIS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM IDENTIFIED (from developer feedback):
  "I don't understand what the value proposition is."
  "Immediately after the first heading, the next thing I see is pricing."

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
  Tensectra is surfacing pricing before the visitor understands the
  product, the audience fit, or the outcomes they will get. This is
  the opposite of Railway's proven flow.

RECOMMENDED TENSECTRA SITE FLOW (inspired by Railway):

  NAV ORDER:
    Product | Courses | For Developers | For Teams | About | Pricing

  HOMEPAGE SECTIONS (proposed order):
    1. Hero       - Bold outcome headline + 1 sentence category label + CTA
    2. What is it - 3-4 core value pillars (what Tensectra actually does)
    3. Who is it for - Developer audience fit + career outcomes
    4. Social proof - Testimonials, graduate outcomes, or community size
    5. Call to action - "Start for free" or "Browse courses"
    NO pricing section on homepage.

  PRICING PAGE (separate, navigated to by choice):
    - Frame pricing as value, not cost ("Invest once, deploy faster forever")
    - Show free or low-cost entry point first
    - Add comparison: "vs. trial-and-error learning"
    - Include outcome metrics next to price ("graduates get X% faster
      at shipping production code")

  COPYWRITING FIXES:
    - Replace product-first language with outcome-first language
      BAD:  "Tensectra offers playbooks and live cohorts"
      GOOD: "Ship production-grade code in weeks, not months"
    - Add a one-liner category label below the hero
      EXAMPLE: "The learning platform built for developers who build real things"
    - Use social proof as a trust bridge before pricing is shown
